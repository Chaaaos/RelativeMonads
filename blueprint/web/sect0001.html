Relative Monads

Basic Definitions

Definition 1 Relative Monads

Given two categories \(\C \) and \(\D \) and a functor \(J : \C \to \D \) (root), a relative monad on \(\D \) over \(\C \) consists in :

  • A function \(T : \C \to \D \) ;

  • A natural transformation \(\eta : J \to T\) (unit) ;

  • For each morphism in \(\D \) of the form \(f : J a \to T b\) (where \(a\) and \(b\) are objects of \(\C \)), a morphism \(\lift {f} : T a \to T b\) (extension /lifting operation) ;

satisfying the following conditions :

  • \(\forall a \in obj_{\C }, (f : J a \to T a), f = \lift {f} \circ \eta _a\) (left unitality)

  • \(\forall a \in obj_{\C }, \lift {(\eta _a)} = \mathbb {1}_{T a}\) (right unitality)

  • \(\forall a, b, c \in obj_{\C }, \)
    \((f : J a \to T a), (g : J b \to T c),\)
    \(\lift {\lift {g}\circ f} = \lift {g} \circ \lift {f} \)

Proposition 2

It follows from the conditions in the definition of a relative monad that :

  • \(T\) defines a functor ;

  • \(\eta \) and \(\lift {·}\) are natural transformations.
